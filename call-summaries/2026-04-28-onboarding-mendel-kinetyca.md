## Summary
Kickoff call to evaluate Mendel's current outbound operation, clarify roles between agency and freelancer, and define what Kinetyca is taking over. The agency runs a "black box" approach (no visibility into emails sent or LinkedIn outreach); the freelancer is fully transparent through SmartLead. Decision made to phase both out and rebuild on owned infrastructure. Outbound performance metrics were presented showing the agency is generating leads but with no transparency. Target market locked: Mexico mid-market + enterprise, CFO-led buying committee. Mendel's two main differentiators called out: real-time spend control BEFORE the transaction and automated tax invoice (CFDI) retrieval — both Mexico-specific edge against global tools.

## Key Discussion Points
- **Current outbound state:** agency = black-box (emails + LinkedIn, no visibility); freelancer = SmartLead-based with full visibility into 7+ campaigns. Aggregate ~31k leads, reply rates 0.36% – 2.82%.
- **Target market for Kinetyca:** mid-market and enterprise in Mexico (90% growth focus), with Argentina and Chile secondary. CFO is the primary decision-maker; Controller / Finance Director / Tax Manager are secondary buyers. Employees (sales reps, drivers, field staff) are end-users but not decision-makers.
- **Product positioning anchors:**
  - Real-time policy engine that blocks transactions BEFORE they happen — competitors (Concur, AMEX, bank cards) detect violations post-hoc.
  - CFDI invoice recovery automated with SAT validation, claimed +30% deductibility lift. No global competitor matches this.
  - One platform covering Cards + Expenses + Reimbursements + Invoice Payments + Travel + ERP (vs. Concur+AMEX fragmentation).
- **Status quo to displace:** employees paying out-of-pocket and waiting for reimbursement; corporate cards reserved for C-levels; SAP Concur + Excel + email + AMEX statements stitched together.
- **Infrastructure decision:** new owned email infrastructure on Zapmail (Growth plan), Scaledmail, Winnr. Mendel to set up workspaces (so they own credentials), Kinetyca to operate.
- **Cadence:** weekly Tuesday 2pm ET / 12pm Mexico for progress + decisions.

## ICP / Positioning Insights
- Alan emphasizes that the buyer is finance, NOT the end-user employee. A long-shot bottom-up path exists via VP Sales for sales force expense friction, but finance team is the deciding authority.
- "Old-school, boring, real economy" companies are the best accounts — logistics, transportation, manufacturing, retail, restaurants/chains, construction, automotive, consumer goods, healthcare/pharma, distribution. Not flashy tech startups.
- 150+ employees in Mexico is the entry threshold (lower than the 500+ baseline noted in the GTM playbook — calibrate down for mid-market reach in Mexico).
- Decision-maker age range: 30-55, but seniority and company fit matter more than age. Bigger companies skew older CFOs.

- New infrastructure on Zapmail + Scaledmail + Winnr (Mendel to create + own credentials).
- Weekly Tuesday 2pm ET sync established.
- Mexico-first build, with Argentina/Chile parallel after Mexico engine is proven.
